METHODOLOGY SKILLS (mandatory application):

### dialog-polish-pass
# Dialog Polish Pass — Zwei-Pass-Strategie für Screenplay-Dialog

## Konzept

Screenplay-Dialog wird in zwei Pässen geschrieben:
1. **Assembly Pass** (Sonnet): Struktur, Scene Headers, Action Lines, erster Dialog-Entwurf
2. **Polish Pass** (Opus): Nur Dialog überarbeiten — Voice Profiles, Subtext, Distinktheit

## Warum zwei Pässe?

### Problem mit einem Pass
Der Assembly-Pass fokussiert auf Struktur: Szenenübergänge, Action-Line-Format, Pacing. Dialog ist dabei "gut genug", aber nicht exzellent. Das LLM balanciert zu viele Aufgaben gleichzeitig.

### Vorteil des Polish-Pass
- **Fokus:** Nur Dialog, keine Strukturänderungen → LLM kann sich voll auf Stimmen konzentrieren
- **Voice Profiles:** Vollständige 9-Dimensionen-Profile werden injiziert (siehe [[voice-profile-injection]])
- **Quality Checks:** 5 explizite Prüfungen pro Durchgang:
  1. Klingt jeder Charakter wie sein Voice Profile?
  2. Gibt es On-the-Nose-Dialog?
  3. Trägt jeder Austausch Subtext?
  4. Wäre der Charakter ohne Namenszusatz erkennbar?
  5. 70/30-Regel: 70% Subtext, max 30% direkt

### Die 70/30-Regel
Professioneller Film-Dialog folgt einer Faustregel: Mindestens 70% des Gesprochenen kommuniziert INDIREKT (durch Subtext, Metapher, Deflection). Maximal 30% darf direkt sein. On-the-Nose-Dialog ("Ich bin traurig") funktioniert in Romanen, aber nicht im Film.

## Modell-Routing

- **Assembly:** Sonnet (analytisch, strukturell, kostengünstig)
- **Polish:** Opus (kreativ, nuanciert, versteht Subtext besser)
- **Routing-Key:** `pass_type="dialog_polish"` → Orchestrator routet automatisch zu Opus

## Token-Budget

STYLE CONTEXT:
Title: Hadji Murat
Genre: historical war tragedy / anti-imperial fable
Tone: tragic and sober, epic distance with intimate moments; violence without glorification; moral ambivalence toward empire and resistance
Style like: Lawrence of Arabia, The Last Duel (2021), The English Patient, War and Peace (Bondarchuk 1966), Come and See, Ran
Themes: sacred hospitality (kunak) as counter-force to imperial violence, man as destroyer of life, The native defender crushed in his proper place

Write scenes S001 through S005 (batch 1/2) in Fountain screenplay format:

[S001] EXT. SUMMER FIELD — EDGE OF HARVESTED RYE — LATE AFTERNOON
Action: A man's boots move through tall grass at the edge of a rye field. The rye has been half-cut; the stubble rows end abruptly where the scythes stopped. He carries a loose nosegay — clover, ox-eye daisies, cornflowers, pale scabious. He stops at a ditch. Below, among the broken weeds and shadow, a single crimson thistle grows in full, violent bloom. He looks at it. He wraps his handkerchief around his hand and climbs into the ditch. He grips the stalk. He pulls. The thistle does not yield. He pulls again — harder — and the stalk bends but does not break. He works at it methodically, breaking the fibers one by one. When he finally wrenches it free, the stalk is frayed, white-fibered, ugly. The flower tilts, already dimming. He holds it beside the nosegay. It does not belong. He lets it drop back into the ditch. He stands for a moment looking down at what he destroyed, then turns and walks away into the rye light.
Emotional core: The reflexive violence of possession — beauty destroyed in the act of reaching for it
Characters: Narrator (Authorial I)
Visual note: Late afternoon amber light; nosegay soft-colored; thistle anomalous crimson; ditch shadowed; the moment is mechanical — no drama, which is the horror.
Emotional beats:
  → The man sees the thistle and reaches for it: Audience feels Unease
  → The thistle resists: Audience feels Respect for the plant
  → The thistle is broken free and diminished: Audience feels Grief
Transition: Hard cut to black; title HADJI MURAT. 1851.

[S002] EXT. BLACK-EARTH FIELD — DIRT ROAD — LATE AFTERNOON
Action: A vast ploughed field. Black earth, uniformly furrowed, stretching to the horizon on every side — not a blade of grass, not a weed. A man walks a dirt road through the center of it. He stops. At the roadside: a Tartar thistle, three-branched. One branch is snapped and hangs at the angle of a broken arm, its flower soiled and pressed into the mud. A second branch bends under its blackened head. The third stands upright — twisted from the base where a cartwheel has crushed through, the stem torqued but unbroken, the dark-edged flower still open at the tip. The man crouches and touches the erect stem with one finger. It does not give. He withdraws his hand, straightens, and looks across the ruined field. He turns and walks. The thistle remains — alone, erect, surrounded by mud.
Emotional core: The refusal to submit — vitality persisting inside destruction
Characters: Narrator (Authorial I)
Visual note: Deep ochre-black earth, grey-white sky, muted crimson thistle. Field uniform to vanishing point; thistle sole vertical irregularity. No music; wind low, thistle sways but does not fall.
Emotional beats:
  → Man walks through total obliteration: Audience feels Oppression
  → He finds the thistle — damaged but upright: Audience feels Recognition — something refuses to die
  → He touches stem; does not yield; withdraws: Audience feels Awe and grief
Transition: Hard cut from the thistle to firelight of Chechen village, sound arriving first

[S003] EXT. MAKHMET AOUL - NARROW SIDE STREET - DUSK
Action: A dry thistle stands at the road's edge — stem broken mid-stalk, head intact, burgundy against grey mud brick. The wind moves it. A horse rounds the corner from the upland track: Hadji Murad, hooded, burka drawn tight, rifle barrel a dark line beneath the wool. One murid rides behind him, close. They hug the left wall, away from the central square. Hadji Murad's eyes move without his head moving. He turns into the side street.
Emotional core: Controlled flight — dignity while hunted
Characters: Hadji Murad, Hadji Murad's Murid (unnamed, active)
Visual note: End-of-day amber light flat against mud brick; burgundy thistle; black burka; murid's dirty white horse; muezzin echo fading.
Emotional beats:
  → The thistle stands alone, undefeated: Audience feels Something irreducible
  → Hadji Murad chooses side street over square: Audience feels Danger and calculated control
Transition: Side street narrows; sound of square falls away.

[S004] EXT. SAKLYA ROOFTOP / PENTHOUSE - MAKHMET - NIGHT FALLING
Action: Hadji Murad stops at the second saklya, cut into the hillside. The penthouse below is empty — a cold firepit, a coil of rope. He looks up. On the flat roof, behind the freshly plastered chimney, the shape of a man under sheepskin, still. Hadji Murad lifts his whip handle and taps the chimney rim twice. The sheepskin shifts. An old man rises: greasy beshmet, nightcap pushed sideways. His eyes go left along the alley, right up the slope, then return.
Emotional core: Sacred debt of hospitality — two men transacting across silence
Characters: Hadji Murad, Hadji Murad's Murid (unnamed, active), Old man on the saklya roof
Visual note: Saklya cut into hillside; chimney white against darkening sky; nightcap canted; murid motionless; last sky-light from west.
Dialog drafts:
  Old man on the saklya roof: "Alone?" (Subtext: Have you brought death to my household?)
  Hadji Murad: "Two." (Subtext: Not a threat — a guest asking the code to hold)
  Old man on the saklya roof: "Come down from the roof. As Allah wills." (Subtext: Under my protection until morning)
Emotional beats:
  → Double tap on chimney — code recognized: Audience feels Relief with dread
  → Old man's silent scan — left, right, back: Audience feels Weight of hospitality's cost
  → 'Two.' — minimum answer: Audience feels Economy as discipline
Transition: Old man descends ladder; alley darkens; smell of warm bread and kizyak smoke; door closes.

[S005] EXT./INT. SADO'S SAKLYA — NIGHT
Action: The compound is dark. A single clay chimney bleeds smoke into a moonless sky. Hadji Murad reins in at the gate, Eldar already off his horse. The old grandfather on the roof stirs under a sheepskin, sits up, recognizes the face below, descends backward down the ladder. Eldar takes the bridle from the old man without a word. Hadji Murad dismounts, favoring his left leg, ducks under the penthouse. A boy freezes in the doorway. Inside, the woman of the house sets three cushions along the front wall with unhurried precision. The rifles and sword of the master hang between two posts; the old man lifts Hadji Murad's burka, rifle, and sword and adds them to that wall.
Emotional core: Refuge received — fragile, contingent on everything unsaid
Characters: Hadji Murad, Old man on the saklya roof, Eldar (Hadji Murad's Murid), Sado's Wife, Sado's Son
Visual note: Single lamp and chimney glow; weapons rack as visual anchor; wife in peripheral light; boy in threshold; Eldar near the door.
Dialog drafts:
  Hadji Murad: "Selaam aleikum." (Subtext: The greeting is a key in a lock)
  Old man on the saklya roof: "Aleikum, selaam." (Subtext: I will ask nothing of what you are running from)
  Old man on the saklya roof: "Run to the mosque. Fetch your father." (Subtext: Sado must be here)
  Sado's Wife: "May thy coming bring happiness." (Subtext: The house accepts it)
  Hadji Murad: "May thy sons live." (Subtext: A thought of Yusuf he cannot stop)
Emotional beats:
  → Recognized in silence, admitted without explanation: Audience feels Relief and unease
  → Eldar displaces old man at bridle: Audience feels Weight of protection
  → Boy stares; HM doesn't look: Audience feels We are watching a legend arrive
Transition: Boy runs; bare feet on packed earth; lamp-light shrinks; mosque door carries into next scene
