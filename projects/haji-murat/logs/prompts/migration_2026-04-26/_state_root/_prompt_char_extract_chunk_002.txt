In front of me to the right of the road I saw some kind of little clump, and drawing nearer I found it was the same kind of thistle as that which I had vainly plucked and thrown away.  This "Tartar" plant had three branches.  One was broken and stuck out like the stump of a mutilated arm.  Each of the other two bore a flower, once red but now blackened. One stalk was broken, and half of it hung down with a soiled flower at its tip.  The other, though also soiled with black mud, still stood erect.
